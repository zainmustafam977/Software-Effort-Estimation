Deeper Research: Metrics to Agile

Mapping traditional software metrics into Scrum iterations.

Function Points Story Points

Historically, an Adjusted Function Point (AdjFP) represented discrete business functionality. In our Scrum model, an Epic with 100 AdjFP can be mapped to 10 Story Points, allowing teams to divide work into 2-week sprints based on historical velocity (e.g., 5 Story Points per sprint).

Simulated Burn-down

Applying the Random Forest model to the Albrecht dataset, a project estimated at 102.4 hours would translate to approximately 1.5 weeks of effort for a 2-person development team (Hamdan & Abdullah), fitting perfectly within the 1-4 week FYP sprint constraints.

10 Advanced Analysis Directives

To elevate this research, the following 10 advanced analytical dimensions should be explored in future modeling:

1. Cross-Validation (K-Fold)

Apply 10-fold cross-validation on the Random Forest models to ensure the effort predictions are robust and not overfitted to a specific subset of the ISBSG or Albrecht data.

2. Hyperparameter GridSearch

Implement GridSearch to dynamically tune tree depth and estimators in the predictive models, squeezing out maximum accuracy for small datasets like Kemerer.

3. Learning Curves

Plot learning curves to determine if acquiring more historical project data (e.g., adding more teams' data over multiple semesters) significantly improves Scrum estimation accuracy.

4. SHAP Value Explainability

Use SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) to crack open the "black box" of the ML models, showing teams exactly *why* a sprint is estimated to take 4 weeks instead of 2 based on specific features.

5. K-Means Project Clustering

Cluster historical projects into "T-Shirt Sizes" (Small, Medium, Large, Epic) using K-Means. This maps perfectly to Agile T-Shirt sizing for high-level backlog grooming.

6. Time-Series Velocity Tracking

Simulate continuous Sprints as a time-series forecast to predict when a team's velocity might dip due to technical debt accumulation over multiple semesters.

7. Feature Interaction Mapping

Generate 2D partial dependence plots to see how Team Size and Total Function Points interact to exponentially increase the risk of missing Sprint deadlines.

8. Residual Error Distribution

Analyze where the models fail worst. If the model severely underestimates highly complex projects, we can flag specific User Stories in Jira as "High Estimation Risk."

9. Algorithmic Complexity Adjustments

Integrate COCOMO II scale factors (e.g., team cohesion, platform volatility) directly into the Scrum velocity modifier to adjust basic statistical estimates dynamically.

10. Agile Risk Assessment Matrix

Develop a probability vs. impact matrix based on the prediction variance of the Random Forest. Sprints with high variance estimates trigger immediate Product Owner review.
