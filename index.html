Deeper Research: Metrics to Agile

Mapping traditional software metrics into Scrum iterations.

Function Points Story Points

Historically, an Adjusted Function Point (AdjFP) represented discrete business functionality. In our Scrum model, an Epic with 100 AdjFP can be mapped to 10 Story Points, allowing teams to divide work into 2-week sprints based on historical velocity (e.g., 5 Story Points per sprint).

Simulated Burn-down

Applying the Random Forest model to the Albrecht dataset, a project estimated at 102.4 hours would translate to approximately 1.5 weeks of effort for a 2-person development team (Hamdan & Abdullah), fitting perfectly within the 1-4 week FYP sprint constraints.

15 Advanced Analysis Directives

To elevate this research, the following 15 advanced analytical dimensions should be explored in future modeling:

1. COCOMO II Calibration

Tune standard algorithmic models to the ISBSG dataset, replacing generic constants with locally derived multi-variable regressions.

2. Story Point Conversion

Develop a statistical mapping matrix to bridge Adjusted Function Points directly into Agile Story Points based on historical sprint velocities.

3. Monte Carlo Risk Simulation

Run 10,000 randomized sprint simulations to establish the 90% confidence interval for on-time delivery across all datasets.

4. Velocity Stability Metrics

Measure the standard deviation of team output over consecutive sprints to pinpoint periods of process instability.

5. Technical Debt Drag

Model how accumulated technical debt exponentially increases future sprint effort and degrades overall Scrum velocity.

6. Defect Density Impact

Correlate pre-release bug counts from Kemerer and Maxwell datasets with total development effort inflation.

7. Requirement Volatility

Analyze effort spikes caused by Product Owner scope changes injected mid-sprint, tracking the ripple effect on downstream tasks.

8. Skill Multiplier Matrix

Adjust raw effort estimations dynamically based on Developer Experience pairings (e.g., Junior/Senior vs. Senior/Senior).

9. Sprint Burn-up Trajectories

Forecast overall project completion dates using predictive burn-up charts mapped against historical data.

10. Use Case Points (UCP)

Compare UCP estimations directly against traditional Lines of Code (LOC) for modern object-oriented projects.

11. Cosmic Function Points

Implement ISO standard Cosmic Function Points (CFP) for sizing real-time and multi-layered software architectures.

12. Deep Learning Estimators

Deploy Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) on the Desharnais dataset to capture non-linear relationships missed by linear regression.

13. Ensemble Regressors

Combine Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and SVMs to create an ultra-resilient master prediction model.

14. Outlier Detection

Use Isolation Forests to automatically identify and strip anomalously delayed historical projects from training data.

15. Bayesian Uncertainty Networks

Map the probabilistic relationships between team size, agile methodology, and final effort to output dynamic confidence scores.
